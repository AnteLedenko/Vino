South America

South America is home to a rapidly growing wine industry, with Argentina and Chile leading the way.

Argentina:

The Mendoza region is the global capital for Malbec, which thrives in the high-altitude vineyards of the Andes. Argentina also produces Torrontés (a fragrant white wine) and Bonarda.

Chile:

Known for Cabernet Sauvignon, Carmenère, and Sauvignon Blanc. The Maipo Valley, Colchagua Valley, and Casablanca Valley are among the top regions. Carmenère, originally from France, has found a new home in Chile.

Uruguay:

Known for Tannat, a robust red wine that has become the country's flagship grape. The Canelones region is key for wine production.

Brazil:

Serra Gaúcha is Brazils main wine region, with a focus on sparkling wines made from Chardonnay and Pinot Noir. Brazil is becoming known for its vibrant wine tourism.
