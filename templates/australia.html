Australia and Oceania

Australia and New Zealand have firmly established themselves as major players in the global wine industry, with distinct styles and varietals.

Australia:

Barossa Valley in South Australia is famous for rich, full-bodied Shiraz (Syrah). Other notable regions include Hunter Valley (known for Semillon) and Margaret River (known for Cabernet Sauvignon and Chardonnay). Australia also excels in Riesling from the Clare Valley and Eden Valley.

Tasmania:

Tasmania is emerging as a producer of cool-climate wines, particularly Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and sparkling wines.

New Zealand:

The country has made a global name for Sauvignon Blanc from the Marlborough region, with its distinctive tropical and citrus flavors. Central Otago is recognized for world-class Pinot Noir.
