Asia

Asias wine industry is still emerging but is rapidly gaining attention.

China:

China has become a major wine producer, particularly for red wines, with Cabernet Sauvignon being the dominant grape. The Ningxia region is considered the "Bordeaux of China" and is producing award-winning wines.

Japan:

Best known for Koshu, a delicate white wine made from the Koshu grape, which is well-suited to Japan's humid climate. The Yamanashi Prefecture is Japans main wine-producing region.

India:

Wine production in India is concentrated in the Nashik Valley, often called "Indias Napa Valley." Sula Vineyards is one of the leading producers, focusing on Shiraz and Chenin Blanc.

Turkey:

With ancient winemaking traditions dating back thousands of years, Turkey produces unique indigenous varieties like Kalecik Karas and Öküzgözü.
