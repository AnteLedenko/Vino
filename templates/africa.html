Africa

Africa's wine industry is centered on South Africa, but other regions are slowly reviving their winemaking traditions.

South Africa:

The Western Cape region, including Stellenbosch, Paarl, and Franschhoek, is the heart of the wine industry. South Africa is known for Pinotage (a cross between Pinot Noir and Cinsault) and Chenin Blanc, called Steen locally.

Morocco:

Wine has been produced here for centuries, but its making a comeback with varieties like Syrah, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Carignan. The Meknes region is particularly important for wine production.

Algeria:

Once the fourth-largest wine producer in the world (during French colonization), Algeria is trying to revive its winemaking with varietals like Cinsault, Grenache, and Carignan.
