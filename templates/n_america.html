North America

North America is dominated by the United States, with Canada and Mexico also making waves.

United States:

California:

The state is home to iconic regions like Napa Valley (famous for Cabernet Sauvignon and Chardonnay) and Sonoma (known for Pinot Noir and Zinfandel). Paso Robles, Santa Barbara, and Mendocino are also rising regions.

Oregon:

Known for Pinot Noir, especially from the Willamette Valley.

Washington State:

Recognized for bold red varietals, particularly Syrah and Cabernet Sauvignon, from regions like Walla Walla and Columbia Valley.

Canada:

Ontarios Niagara Peninsula and British Columbias Okanagan Valley are leading regions, known for Ice Wine (made from frozen grapes). Ontario is famous for Riesling and Vidal Blanc.

Mexico:

The Valle de Guadalupe in Baja California is the heart of Mexican winemaking, with bold reds like Nebbiolo, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Syrah gaining recognition.
