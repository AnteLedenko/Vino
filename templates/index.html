What is Wine?

Typically made by fermenting grapes, wine, commonly referred to as "vino," is a popular alcoholic beverage consumed worldwide.

As one of the oldest alcoholic beverages, wine has a lengthy and distinguished history. It is also believed that many of the most notable historical figures have preferred wine as their beverage of choice.

There are numerous types of wine, and categorizing them can be complex due to variations in grape varieties, production methods, and regional classifications.But we will just cover the primary category, which we have divided into seven sorts of wine.

Main Categories

  • Red Wines
  • White Wines
  • Rose Wines
  • Orange Wines
  • Sparkling Wines
  • Dessert Wines
  • Fortified Wines

Red Wines

The flavorful favorite red wine actually ferments with the grape skins to provide its deep color and flavor.

The majority of purple or red grapes actually produce greenish-white wine; the pigments called anthocyanins, which are present in the grape skin, are what give wine its beloved red color.

A bitter, slightly dry aftertaste that is often referred to chocolaty or occasionally oaky is left in your tongue after enjoying a sip of a really superb red wine.

Red wine grape varieties include Pinot noir, Cabernet Sauvignon, Malbec, Tempranillo, Merlot, Bobal, Cinsaut and Zinfandel.

White Wines

It can be made with both black and white grapes, but just like red wine, the final product's flavor profile is mostly determined by the type of grape used and the fermenting method employed.

White wine is produced by quickly pressing the grapes and extracting the liquid from the skins. As a result, the finished product has low tannins and a crisp, fresh flavor from its acidity.

White wine grape varieties include Chardonnay, Sauvignon blanc, Muscat, Müller-Thurgau, Chenin blanc, Trebbiano, Viognier, Sauvignon gris, Pinot Meunier, Grenache Gris and more.

Rose Wines

The most distinctive feature of rosé wine is its distinct blush-pink color, which makes it somewhat sweeter than both red and white wines. The color of rosé wine is mostly caused by black grape skins, but not enough to make it a red wine.

Rosé wine can be made from any type of black grape, and the final flavor of the wine will naturally depend on the grape variety and growing region.

The most well-known rose wines are Provence (French), Rosado (Spanish), and Rosato (Italian).

Orange Wines

Orange wine, sometimes known as "Amber wine," comes in a variety of colors, from dark straw to dark gold, making its name "orange" a little misleading.

While produced in a way similar to that of both red and rosé wine... These wines are made from white grapes, but before pressing, the skins are allowed to soak.

Due to this procedure and the extended soaking of the skins, orange wine has a distinctly tannic flavor and is typically served dry.

Sparkling Wines

Sparkling wines frequently have their origins in their names. In order to produce CO2 bubbles that make the wine effervescent and give it a light, sparkling quality, sparkling wine is fermented in a bottle.

Sparkling wines are sweetened and flavored, usually using white wines, though sparkling rosé and occasionally red wines are also available.

Fortified Wines

A wine known as fortified wine is created by adding distilled alcohol, most commonly brandy, to wine.

These wines are typically served toward the end of a meal and are typically fairly high in sugar and alcohol content.

Among the well-known fortified wines are Port, Commandaria, Sherry, Madeira, Marsala, and the aromatized wine Vermouth.

Dessert Wines

Due to the fact that they are both on the sweeter side, dessert wines and fortified wines are often mixed.

Many grape varietals, such as Muscat, Ortega, Huxelrebe, etc., are used to make dessert wine. These grape varietals are ideal for use in dessert wines since they naturally produce a lot more sugar than many others.

However, the process of manufacturing dessert wine also involves the manual addition of more alcohol and sugar. Ice wine, noble rot, and raisin wine are a few of the well-liked dessert wines.
