Europe

Europe is the most established wine-producing region in the world, with centuries-old traditions that have shaped the norms for winemaking around the world.

France:

Known for regions like Bordeaux, Burgundy, and Champagne. Bordeaux is famous for Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, and Cabernet Franc, while Burgundy is home to Pinot Noir and Chardonnay. Champagne is known for its world-renowned sparkling wines.

Italy:

Italy has over 20 wine regions. Tuscany is famous for Chianti (Sangiovese), while Piedmont is known for Barolo and Barbaresco (Nebbiolo). The Veneto region produces Prosecco and Amarone.

Spain:

Known for Rioja (Tempranillo), Ribera del Duero, and Sherry from Jerez. The Priorat region is also notable for its bold red wines.

Germany:

Renowned for Riesling, especially in the Mosel and Rheingau regions. German wines are known for their balance of acidity and sweetness.

Portugal:

Douro Valley produces Port wine (fortified wine), and Vinho Verde is a light, refreshing white wine. The Alentejo region is known for bold red wines.
